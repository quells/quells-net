Chemistry Is Everywhere


Info


Showing the ubiquity of chemistry through photography.

Clockwise from top left:

A homemade arc lamp, showing the light given off by the ionization of air as electrical current jumps between two carbon electrodes.

A conventional fluorescent lamp with a geometric shade pattern. Such lamps are a more controlled version of an arc lamp.

A sunset over the Pacific Ocean captured on black and white film with a SLR camera. It was projected on to two sheets of paper impregnated with homemade silver nitrate photo emulsion. One sheet was evenly coated, while the other was a much rougher application. The two photos were then pasted together.
