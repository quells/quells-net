How does this relate to thermodynamics?

In any kind of light bulb, the first law of thermodynamics can be said to be the reason for their functionality. The equation for the first law is ∆U = Q - W. This means that the change in internal energy (U) of a system is equal to the heat energy added (Q) minus the work done (W) by the system. In a light bulb, the heat energy added is the electricity, and the work done is the electrical resistance of the material that the electricity is run through. The resistance is always significantly lower than the initial input, so the internal energy increases. This then needs to dissipate, and that is done in the form of visible light.
