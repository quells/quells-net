How do Light Emitting Diodes Work?

In an LED, or ‘light emitting diode,’ electricity is run through a semiconductor that has holes in it. When electrons run through the semiconductor and hit a hole, they lose energy and emit a photon – light. Because there are no moving parts, LED’s tend to have extremely long life spans, generally tens of thousands of hours – usually at least twenty times longer than incandescent bulbs. However, LED’s are very tiny, so many of them must be used to generate the same amount of light as a normal bulb. This is counteracted, though, by their very low energy requirements – for eighteen, less than a watt per hour.
