Hue Color Picker

Hue is a gesture based color selection tool for iPhone, iPod touch, and iPad that requires iOS 6.0 or greater. Hue is optimized for iPhone 5. Hue will not run on the original iPad or older models of iPhone and iPod touch.

Hue was not made to solve every problem that you may encounter when selecting a color. It was made to make selecting colors a more fun and engaging activity.

Hue renders all text in Futura.

Features:

  • 24-bit decimal RGB color selection mode
  • 24-bit hexadecimal RGB color selection mode
  • Hue, Saturation, and Value selection mode
  • Copying to the pasteboard (formatted for current color mode)
  • iPhone and iPod touch: Quickly switch between two colors to compare them
  • iPad: View two colors side-by-side

NOT Features of Hue:

  • CMYK, Pantone, and other print color spaces. It is impossible to ensure that what you see on an iOS device’s screen will match what comes out of your printer. Thus, Hue makes no attempt to do so.

FAQ

  • How do I use it?
    • Swipe to change values. Tap the bottom to change modes. Tap twice to switch colors.
  • What’s the difference between decimal and hexadecimal?
    • They are simply different numbering systems that display the same information. Decimal is the numbering system that most people are familiar with: 0-9. Hexadecimal is the numbering system commonly used in computing and extends decimal to include a-f (10-15 in decimal).

Privacy Policy

Hue does not collect any user data, anonymous or otherwise. Color data is sent to and stored at Apple’s iCloud servers for syncing purposes.
